IBM Digital App Builder


IBM Cloud is a platform that offers developers, backend services on the cloud to build their digital apps on. With increasing penetration of technology into business world, businesses now want to quickly create powerful and rich apps for their customers and sellers to interact with. Not having a way to design the apps using their backend services, IBM felt a gap and a need to introduce a product that lets developers consume the IBM Cloud services through an app builder interface. The output? IBM Digital App Builder.

Background


Before building the tool, IBM had invested into a research project of creating a web-based low-code mobile app builder which utilized an IBM Cloud service for providing the back-end capability. However, the project didn’t take off and was deprecated. Due to the changing mobile app development landscape in recent years, many rapid app builders have come up in the market. IBM, despite having Cloud services, platform and infrastructure to support scalable mobile development, didn’t have an efficient way to consume those services, thus decreasing their adoption.


Gartner Review of 2018 highlighted an opportunity in IBM’s high-productivity app-building capability for which IBM had no tool to offer. So, a business decision was made to plug the gap by offering a tool that would empower 'Citizen' mobile developers create mobile apps, quickly, by consuming IBM Cloud services as the back-end.

Research


Mobile-app Development Lifecycle


To study the process of how a mobile app gets developed in an organisation, I undertook an ethnographic research with the IBM Garage team in Bengaluru and uncovered following insights:

  1. The development process is only done by expert programmers by using the IBM Cloud SDK to utilise the Cloud services.
  2. The data held by the organisation is extensive and private. They may or may not allow the developer to use it.
  3. The developers have to go through a time consuming and tedious process for distributing the developed app for testing and launch.


The IBM Cloud Mobile Foundation


The IBM Cloud Mobile Foundation provides a framework that enables mobile app developers to use capabilities such as development, testing, back-end connections, push notifications, offline mode, update, security, analytics, monitoring, and application publishing. Mobile Foundation is platform agnostic and works with all major platforms such as iOS, Android, Windows and Web. Using IBM's Mobile Foundation as the only back-end for building apps was one of the requirements for the new tool. Learn more about the Mobile Foundation product features.

Hills

After working on a IBM Enterprise Design Thinking workshop with 15 participants including sponsored users, we finally created three 'Hills'. Hills are phased goals of a project that focus on the achievable outcomes


WHO WHAT WOW
1 A citizen developer can build a B2E app for multiple channels using building block components and services without writing a single line of code
2 A citizen developer can create and update AI capabilities used in her app through an updating AI model without data science knowledge
3 An app developer can share & expand the functionality of a basic Studio app with his IDE of choice without writing app basics

The Launcher

The IBM Digital App Builder launcher is the starting point for the process of app creation. It let's you create new apps or open existing App Builder apps for various platforms such as iOS, Android, Web and Progressive Web-apps.

The launcher provides a set of templates to get started with building apps quickly without starting the process from scratch.

Switch your workflow from the Sidebar

The sidebar on the IBM Digital App Builder is the universal navigation to switch from one section of the tool to another. It has sections for creating the app, setup app preferences and a console log. Sections include a Design workbench to design the apps,

a Data viewer to add and use data into your apps, a Watson sections to add AI capability into the app and an add-on section to integrate services that increase user engagement.

Design apps on Workbench

The Workbench is the most important section in the IBM Digital App Builder which lets the user design, preview and publish their app by dragging and dropping controls on a mobile screen canvas.

Add data to your apps

The Data section lets you create a dataset out of any datasource. Since the dataset is created through Mobile Foundation, it requires a little knowledge of Adapters to add and integrate datasets into your app.

Integrate Artifical Intelligence

The Watson section enables you to integrate AI capabilities into your app. It currently provides you the facility to create, test and deploy chatbots into your apps with IBM Watson Assistant. You can also enhance your app by adding an image recognition capability through IBM Visual Recognition service.
