Overview

The current lifestyle patterns among young working professionals living in Indian cities is leading to various health problems such as high-BP, obesity, diabetes, etc. However, every problem is tied to a lot of variables in the system such as individuals, organisations, governments, etc. which affect their health directly or indirectly. The project aims to study the ecosystem around the user, his behaviours and his needs, to provide necessary design intervention for a healthy life.

Research

REPORT

In order to verify whether the problem is serious, we looked at news reports, articles and research papers to find out the stats and to understand the gravity of this problem. The findings from the study suggested that lifestyle diseases are emerging as one of the serious health problem around the world, especially among young professionals in India.

UN SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT GOAL

The United Nations - Sustainable Development Goal 3 targets to ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all at all ages. The target in context, by 2030, reduce by one third premature mortality from noncommunicable diseases through prevention and treatment and promote mental health and well-being

Persona

For the qualitative research, we prepared a questionnaire and interviewed 7 participants to get a first-hand perspective of the activities, causes, effects and the stakeholders around the user. It lead me to narrow down to one major persona of a young working professional to go ahead and design for them.

ECOSYSTEM

The ecosystem around a young working professional talks about his interactions and behaviours with things, individuals and organisations in the system. It connects the user with the other stakeholders in the system and their role.
